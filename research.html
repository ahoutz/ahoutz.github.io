A Systematic Analysis of NGDP Targeting (preparing for submission)

Abstract: Proponents claim that if monetary policy followed an NGDP targeting rule, economic performance under shocks would improve compared to the current practice of inflation targeting. Inflation would stabilize, economic performance would stabilize, welfare would improve, and the zero lower-bound problem would be alleviated. I analyze these claims in two ways. First, I demonstrate analytically that while economic output would be more stable, inflation will be much more volatile. Then, I numerically show that welfare outcomes are shock-dependent. Lastly, I find that NGDP targeting exacerbates, rather than alleviates, the zero lower-bound problem.
